Introduction

Artifact 2 required us to discuss the effects of scientific advancements on human perception of time. This artifact was to be produced in groups, and my group decided to work on 'Effects of Caffeine and Alcohol on Human Perception of Time'. Along with Grady, I set out to work on the script of the podcast and immersed myself into research papers from scientists like Ferre and Lapp. Several revisions and some peer review sessions later, we were finally ready with the podcast script. FINAL DRAFT

The final draft had a lot of fillers moved out and some unnecessary sections deleted. It also contained some brief final instructions for the length of each section of the podcast. This was the document that we used during the final production of the artifact.

Goal
  • The goal of this artifact was to show how the recent introduction of some technology had affected human perception of time.
  • This artifact needed the oral method of communication to be used extensively throughout in a way that is most appealing to the audience.

Argument
  • Along with some of the other students enrolled in this course, I argued on how consumption of alcohol and caffeine (recently introduced products as a result of advancement / development in science) can affect the human perception of time.
  • We gathered information from papers like “Psychopharmacological effects of alcohol on time perception” and “Alcohol and Caffeine: The Perfect Storm” and mentioned quotes from scientists like Ferre and Lapp to add credibility to our argument.
Audience
  • The audience of this artifact is everyone who consumes caffeine / alcohol products on regular basis, in particular, and everyone who might be interested to know more about this field, in general.
  • This artifact makes my choice of audience explicit by informing the listeners what effects can some of the daily-use products have on the human body and brain and how can certain chemicals play with how a brain perceives time.
  • This artifact also starts with scenes that might be quite relatable to the intended audience, thus stating the intended audience once again.
Genre / Media
  • The genre / media used in this artifact is the podcast solely.
  • This artifact called for extensive usage of oral communication skills and also some knowledge of how we can play with background music to have different kinds of impact on the minds of the listeners.
  • For instance, my group used the background music in this artifact to our advantage in a way that it excites the senses of the listener at the most critical moments of the podcast, thus encouraging their minds to lay emphasis on the most important information.
Intended Revision
  • If I had more time for revision, I might have worked on getting a couple of guest speakers who might be expert in their fields for a quick discussion in the podcast.
  • Not only might this have allowed us to add credibility to the overall artifact but this might have also allowed us to have different perspectives for the same problem, thus also inviting the listeners to think along with the speakers.
