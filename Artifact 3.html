Final Submission
How Did We Prepare for Podcast?

The format of a presentation, or the steps in which facts are laid in a presentation, is often the deciding factor in does your audience actually comprehend from what you just said. This is something that my group was concerned about before we actually begun working on this artifact, and as a result, we formulated a five-step approach to laying our facts down while discussing how the current Time Symmetry holds the entire Standard Model of universe together:

  • Discussion on Parity Symmetry
  • Discussion on Charge Symmetry
  • Discussion on Time Symmetry (Independently)
  • Explanation of Standard Model and How Does It Matter In Our Discussion
  • Violations of Symmetries
  • Elaboration on How the Current Standard Model is Held Together by Charge-Parity-Time Symmetry
  • Overall Review

Just like our presentation, we wanted to move in steps while preparing our artifact. As a result, our script preparation stage was also broken down into several stages:

DRAFT - 0

This draft had an overall outline for how we shall proceed in our presentation. We used it more like a guideline while working on our next draft.

DRAFT - 1

Draft 1 was a result of extensive research done by the entire team on our topic of discussion. We used the previous draft and worked on it by adding in more information and the sources we gathered the information from. At this point, we were confident that this is what presented our overall discussion in text.

DRAFT - 2

Draft 2 was the product of several rounds of feedback exchange with the course professor and everyone else we knew. We incorporated their suggestions, adding in a bit more information, thus making the script apparently absolute in itself.

FINAL DRAFT

Coming right next was the rehearsal phase, where we realized that our presentation was exceeding the time limit by about 12 minutes. As a result, we removed some of the unnecessary / not-so-relevant information from the script and added in a few in-presentation cues to help everyone stay connected with the ongoing discussion.

Goal
  • The goal of this artifact was to present a scientific topic that was related to time in a way that even those who were not quite acquainted with the behind-the-scenes science could comprehend our discussion well.
  • This artifact was to be in the form of a group presentation, requiring usage of all the five modes of WOVEN communication.
Argument
  • We decided to go ahead with a presentation on how the Charge-Parity-Time symmetry holds the current Standard Model of universe together and how time cannot flow in backward direction because of it.
  • Apart from using just a PowerPoint Presentation, we decided to make everything a bit more interactive by inviting the audience to participate in some fun activities that we used to explain some otherwise-hard-to-comprehend concepts.
Audience
  • The audience of this artifact is everyone who might be interested in knowing more about how time flows and what stops it from changing its direction.
  • Neither do they need to be experts in the field of quantum mechanics, nor do they need to have any prior knowledge to enjoy the overall presentation.
  • We made our choice of audience explicit in our presentation by starting from Ground 0, introducing new information to the audience, establishing the facts and explaining with the usage of several metaphors, inviting them to participate in an activity, and finally moving to the deep end of the discussion, thus making sure that even those who had no previous knowledge of the subject could comprehend what we debated.
Genre / Media
  • The genre / media used in this artifact is an interactive presentation.
  • The presentation was aided by Microsoft PowerPoint, interactive activities, and some artifact-related thought sessions.
  • This enabled us to use all the five forms of WOVEN communication - written, oral, visual, electronic, and non-verbal - to better display our thoughts and ideas and convey them to the audience, while encouraging them to get involved in the overall discussion by inviting them to act along with us as moved deeper into the discussion.

Intended Revision
  • If I had more time for revision, I might have worked a bit more on gathering information from direct sources instead of third-party sources, thus increasing the overall credibility of our presentation.
  • I might also have worked with the team to organize another activity that might have helped us explain some of the symmetry violations - another pretty complicated topic.
